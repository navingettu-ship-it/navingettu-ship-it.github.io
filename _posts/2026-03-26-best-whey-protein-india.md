India's whey protein market has a serious quality problem. Multiple independent tests over the years have found products spiked with cheap amino acids to inflate apparent protein content (amino acid spiking), products with far less protein per scoop than labelled, and products contaminated with heavy metals.

This doesn't mean you can't buy good protein in India — you absolutely can. It means you need to know what to look for and which brands have earned trust through third-party verification.

---

## Whey Protein 101: Concentrate vs Isolate vs Hydrolysate

**Whey Concentrate (WPC):** The least processed form. Typically 70–80% protein by weight, with remaining calories from fat and lactose. Less expensive. Slightly slower digesting. Good choice for most people if lactose-tolerant.

**Whey Isolate (WPI):** Further processed to remove most fat and lactose. Typically 90%+ protein by weight. Better for lactose-sensitive individuals. Higher protein per scoop. Costs more.

**Whey Hydrolysate:** Pre-digested isolate — broken into smaller peptides for fastest absorption. Most expensive, often bitter. Marginally faster post-workout absorption. Not worth the premium for most people.

**The practical choice:** Whey concentrate if you're healthy, lactose-tolerant, and budget-conscious. Isolate if you're lactose-sensitive or want maximum protein per calorie.

---

## How to Spot a Fake or Underdosed Product

**Check protein per 100g of powder, not per scoop.** Scoop sizes vary wildly — some brands use 50g scoops and claim "25g protein per serving." A legitimate whey protein should deliver **70–85g of protein per 100g of powder** (concentrate) or **85–90g+** (isolate). If the number is below 70g per 100g, you're either buying an inferior blend or a spiked product.

**Look for third-party certification.** Labdoor (USA) and Informed Sport test for label accuracy and banned substances. AS-IT-IS Nutrition uses Labdoor certification — one of the few Indian brands that does.

**Avoid extremely cheap products.** Genuine whey protein costs money. If a 2kg product is priced suspiciously low compared to established brands, it's likely adulterated.

## How Much Protein Do You Actually Need?

| Goal | Daily protein target |
|---|---|
| Sedentary adult | 0.8g per kg body weight |
| Active adult / regular exercise | 1.2–1.6g per kg |
| Muscle building | 1.6–2.2g per kg |
| Cutting (preserving muscle in deficit) | 2.0–2.4g per kg |

Most Indians significantly undereat protein — the average Indian diet is heavily carbohydrate-dominant. A 70kg person building muscle needs 112–154g of protein per day; most are getting 50–70g. A whey supplement covering 25–50g of that gap is genuinely useful.

## Timing: When to Take Protein

**The "anabolic window" is overblown.** Research shows that total daily protein intake matters far more than precise timing. That said:

- **Post-workout:** A serving within 1–2 hours of training is sensible and practical
- **Breakfast:** Adding protein to a typically low-protein Indian breakfast (idli, poha, paratha) is often the highest-leverage timing for most people
- **Before bed:** Casein protein (from milk or cottage cheese / paneer) is slower-digesting and supports overnight muscle repair — whey works too if casein isn't practical
