Zinc is one of the most important trace minerals in the body — involved in immune function, wound healing, testosterone production, skin health, and protein synthesis. It's also one of the more common deficiencies in India, particularly in those eating predominantly plant-based diets, since zinc from plant sources (phytate-rich grains and legumes) absorbs at a fraction of the rate of zinc from meat and shellfish.

The supplement market is flooded with zinc products, but most use cheap, poorly-absorbed forms. Here's how to choose correctly.

---

## Zinc Forms: What Actually Absorbs

**Zinc oxide** — the cheapest, most common form. Poorly absorbed. Found in many low-cost multivitamins. Avoid for targeted zinc supplementation.

**Zinc sulphate** — slightly better than oxide but still causes significant stomach upset for many people. Used in medical settings for deficiency treatment.

**Zinc gluconate** — moderate bioavailability, gentler on the stomach. Common in lozenges.

**Zinc picolinate** — zinc bound to picolinic acid, which acts as a natural chelating agent. Consistently shows superior absorption in comparative studies. The preferred form for daily supplementation.

**Zinc bisglycinate** — zinc bound to glycine. Similar bioavailability to picolinate, gentle on the stomach. A solid alternative.

**The short version:** Always choose picolinate or bisglycinate. The price difference over oxide is minimal; the absorption difference is significant.

---

## What Zinc Actually Does

**Immunity:** Zinc is essential for the development and function of immune cells. Even mild deficiency impairs immune response. This is the most evidence-backed use case for supplementation.

**Hair and skin:** Zinc deficiency directly causes hair loss — it's one of the most frequently overlooked nutritional causes of alopecia in India. Zinc is also essential for skin cell regeneration and wound healing.

**Testosterone:** Zinc is a cofactor in testosterone synthesis. Deficiency reduces testosterone levels; correction in deficient individuals restores them. It does not boost testosterone above normal in people with adequate zinc.

**Taste and smell:** Zinc is required for taste and smell receptor function — a fact that became widely known during the COVID-19 pandemic, where zinc deficiency correlated with anosmia.

## How Much Do You Need?

| Group | Recommended daily intake (elemental zinc) |
|---|---|
| Adult men | 11 mg |
| Adult women | 8 mg |
| Pregnant women | 11–13 mg |
| Vegetarians/vegans | +50% above RDA (lower absorption from plant foods) |

Supplemental doses of 15–30mg elemental zinc are typical. Don't go significantly higher without medical supervision — excessive zinc (above 40mg/day long-term) can interfere with copper absorption and cause deficiency.

**Always take zinc with food** — zinc on an empty stomach commonly causes nausea.

## A Note on Zinc and Copper Balance

Long-term zinc supplementation (especially at higher doses) can deplete copper, since the two minerals compete for absorption. If you're taking zinc daily for more than a few months, consider a supplement that includes a small amount of copper (1–2mg), or ensure your diet includes copper-rich foods (nuts, seeds, legumes, dark chocolate).
