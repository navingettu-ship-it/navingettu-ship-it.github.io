Vitamin B12 deficiency is one of the most common and most underdiagnosed nutritional deficiencies in India — and it disproportionately affects vegetarians and vegans. B12 is found almost exclusively in animal products: meat, fish, eggs, and dairy. A vegetarian diet that's low in dairy can leave you chronically deficient for years before symptoms become obvious.

The consequences aren't minor. Untreated B12 deficiency causes irreversible nerve damage, cognitive decline, fatigue, and anaemia. Supplementation is simple and cheap — the problem is choosing the right form.

---

## Methylcobalamin vs Cyanocobalamin: Why It Matters

This is the single most important decision when buying a B12 supplement.

**Cyanocobalamin** is the synthetic form found in most cheap supplements. Your body has to convert it to methylcobalamin before it can use it — a conversion that's less efficient in people with certain genetic variants (MTHFR mutations, which are common). It also releases a tiny amount of cyanide during processing, which the body clears easily at normal doses, but which makes it a suboptimal choice.

**Methylcobalamin** is the active, bioavailable form — the form your body actually uses. It requires no conversion, is retained in tissues longer, and is the form with the strongest evidence for neurological protection. For anyone buying B12 in India, methylcobalamin is the right choice. The price difference is small.

---

## How Much Do You Need?

| Situation | Daily dose |
|---|---|
| General maintenance (non-vegetarian) | 250–500 mcg |
| Vegetarian or limited dairy intake | 500–1000 mcg |
| Confirmed deficiency | 1000–1500 mcg daily (or doctor-prescribed injections) |
| Absorption issues (older adults, digestive conditions) | 1000–2000 mcg |

B12 is water-soluble and non-toxic at high doses — excess is simply excreted. Higher doses are a practical way to compensate for variable absorption.

## Signs You May Be B12 Deficient

- Persistent fatigue and low energy despite adequate sleep
- Tingling or numbness in hands and feet
- Brain fog, poor memory, difficulty concentrating
- Pale or yellowish skin
- Mouth ulcers or a swollen, inflamed tongue
- Low mood or depression

Many of these symptoms develop slowly over years — B12 deficiency is often missed until it becomes severe. If you're vegetarian and haven't supplemented consistently, a blood test (serum B12) is worth doing. Most labs in India charge ₹300–800 for it.

---

## How Long Before You See Results?

Energy and cognitive improvements typically begin within **2–4 weeks** of consistent supplementation in deficient individuals. Neurological symptoms (tingling, numbness) take longer — sometimes **3–6 months** — and severe nerve damage may be only partially reversible. Earlier intervention gives better outcomes.
