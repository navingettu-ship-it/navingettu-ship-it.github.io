Biotin is one of the best-selling supplements in India — and one of the most heavily marketed. Walk into any pharmacy or scroll through Amazon India and you'll find dozens of brands claiming to stop hair fall, boost thickness, and grow longer hair in weeks.

The reality is more nuanced. Here's what the evidence actually says, what dose makes sense, and which products deliver real biotin content at a fair price.

---

## Does Biotin Actually Work for Hair?

The honest answer: **it depends on why your hair is struggling.**

Biotin deficiency causes hair loss. Correcting a deficiency restores hair growth. That part is well-established.

The problem is that true biotin deficiency is rare in people eating a normal diet — biotin is found in eggs, nuts, seeds, and most cooked meats, and your gut bacteria also produce it. Most people taking biotin supplements are not actually deficient.

**What the evidence shows for non-deficient people:**
- Studies in people with confirmed biotin deficiency show clear benefit
- Studies in people without deficiency show modest or inconsistent results
- Despite this, many users report subjective improvement in hair quality and nail strength

The most honest framing: biotin is low-risk, inexpensive, and worth trying if you're experiencing hair thinning or slow growth — especially if your diet is restricted or you've been under chronic stress (which can deplete B vitamins).

---

## What Dose Do You Need?

The recommended adequate intake for adults is just **30 mcg per day** — easily met through diet. Supplements typically provide 1000 mcg to 10,000 mcg (10 mg).

The higher doses used in supplements are not dangerous — biotin is water-soluble and excess is excreted. But there's no strong evidence that 10,000 mcg outperforms 1000 mcg for hair growth. The high-dose trend is primarily a marketing construct.

**One important caution:** High-dose biotin (above 5 mg/day) can interfere with several laboratory blood tests, including thyroid function tests and troponin (cardiac marker). Always inform your doctor if you're taking high-dose biotin before any blood work.

## What Else Affects Hair Health?

Biotin is one piece of the puzzle. Other common, addressable causes of hair thinning and hair fall in India:

**Iron deficiency / anaemia** — extremely common among Indian women. Low ferritin (stored iron) is one of the most consistent nutritional causes of hair loss. Get a serum ferritin test (not just haemoglobin) if you suspect this.

**Vitamin D deficiency** — strongly linked to hair follicle health. Hair follicles have Vitamin D receptors, and deficiency disrupts the hair growth cycle.

**Zinc deficiency** — affects scalp health and follicle function. More common with vegetarian diets.

**Protein intake** — hair is made of keratin, a protein. Chronically low protein intake (common in Indian vegetarian diets focused on rice and dal without adequate protein complementing) can directly limit hair growth.

**Chronic stress** — triggers telogen effluvium, a diffuse hair shedding that typically resolves once the stressor is addressed.

If you've been taking biotin for 3+ months with no improvement, consider investigating these other causes rather than simply increasing your biotin dose.

## How Long to See Results?

Hair growth is slow — the active growth phase (anagen) cycles over months. Realistic timelines:

- **Nail strength:** 4–8 weeks
- **Hair texture and reduced breakage:** 8–12 weeks
- **Visible length or density change:** 3–6 months of consistent use
