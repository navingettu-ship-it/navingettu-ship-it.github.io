Collagen is the most abundant protein in the human body — the structural scaffolding of your skin, joints, bones, tendons, and connective tissue. After your mid-20s, your body produces progressively less of it, which contributes to skin losing elasticity, joints becoming less cushioned, and recovery from exercise taking longer.

The Indian market for collagen supplements has exploded, driven largely by skincare interest. But the science behind oral collagen supplementation is actually more solid than most beauty trends. Here's what genuinely works.

---

## Does Oral Collagen Actually Work?

A reasonable question — if you eat protein, your digestive system breaks it into amino acids. Why would collagen peptides behave differently?

The answer is two-fold. First, collagen hydrolysate (the processed form used in supplements) breaks down into small peptides — particularly hydroxyproline-containing dipeptides — that research shows are absorbed intact and accumulate preferentially in skin and joint tissue. Second, these peptides appear to act as signalling molecules that stimulate your own collagen-producing cells (fibroblasts) to increase production.

**The evidence:**
- Multiple RCTs show improved skin hydration, elasticity, and reduced wrinkle depth with 2.5–10g collagen peptides per day over 8–12 weeks
- Studies in athletes show reduced joint pain with 10g collagen peptides + Vitamin C taken before exercise
- Evidence for hair growth is more preliminary but promising

This isn't marketing — it's replicated clinical data. Collagen supplementation is one of the better-supported cosmetic supplement categories.

---

## Marine vs Bovine Collagen

**Marine collagen** (from fish skin/scales) is predominantly **Type I collagen** — the type most abundant in skin. It has slightly smaller peptide size, which may improve absorption. It also tends to be more sustainable and is acceptable to people who avoid beef.

**Bovine collagen** (from cow hide/bones) contains both **Type I and Type III collagen** — Type III is present in skin, blood vessels, and internal organs. A broader spectrum but with slightly larger peptide size.

**For skin and hair:** Marine collagen is typically the better choice.
**For joints and gut health:** Bovine Type I and III has a broader base of evidence.

Both work. Marine is the most popular choice in India's current market, and the evidence base is solid.

---

## Vitamin C Is Not Optional

Collagen synthesis requires Vitamin C as a mandatory cofactor. Without adequate Vitamin C, your body cannot properly assemble collagen chains regardless of how much collagen you're consuming. Always take your collagen supplement alongside a Vitamin C source — either a supplement that includes it, or a glass of amla juice, citrus fruit, or guava (all excellent natural Vitamin C sources).

## How Long to See Results?

Realistic timelines from clinical studies:
- **Skin hydration:** 4–6 weeks
- **Skin elasticity and reduced fine lines:** 8–12 weeks
- **Joint comfort:** 8–12 weeks of consistent use
- **Hair and nail strength:** 3–4 months

Collagen is not an overnight fix. Consistency is the single biggest predictor of results — daily use for at least 3 months is the minimum fair evaluation period.

---

## Who Should Avoid Collagen Supplements?

- Those with fish allergies (marine collagen is fish-derived)
- Those with religious dietary restrictions around beef (bovine collagen)
- People on blood-thinning medications — check with a doctor, as some marine peptides may have mild anticoagulant effects
